Small Town Roots About Me

I decided to pursue a career in business while in high school, working on the family ranch. I learned to love management, decision-making, and problem solving. I focused my interest on finance at BYU, and have found that corporate finance is a fantastic way to engage in high-level problem-solving daily.


In my time at BYU and various work/internship opportunities, I been exposed to accounting processes, programming languages (including VBA, SQL, and Tableau), and financial analysis. Most importantly, I have learned to gather, review, and analyze financial information that I can translate into valuable insights. In addition to developing technical skills, I have learned to think critically, solve problems creatively, and communicate ideas effectively.


I am always trying to learn something new and become 1% better every day.
